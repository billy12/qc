## New Findings — Fill Homework Gaps from `PROPOSAL_PLANNING.md`

### Frontier Family Park layout (Pillar 1 homework)
No formal "event site map" was found publicly, but the park's actual footprint is now
confirmed via the official page and ABC15:
- 85 acres total
- 5-acre fishing lake as a central landmark
- 30,000 sq ft inclusive playground with splash pad
- 3 multipurpose fields, 6 baseball/softball fields, 24 pickleball courts, 2 sand
  volleyball courts, 2 basketball courts
- Illuminated walking path + soft-surface trail, 10 picnic ramadas, restrooms,
  concession areas, ample parking
- Source: https://www.queencreekaz.gov/FrontierFamilyPark

This is enough to sanity-check the circular-journey concept: the park is large and
multi-zoned (not a single open field), so a genuine loop past fields/courts/lake is
physically plausible — but you'll still want to confirm exactly which
sub-area Spring Into QC actually occupies (likely not the full 85 acres for a 4-hour
event).

### Attendance & vendor structure (Pillar 2 homework)
Found real operational numbers not previously in the deck:
- **3,500+ attendees** at the most recent Spring Into QC
- Vendor booths are **10 ft × 10 ft**, fees **$75–$125** depending on type
- Vendor/sponsorship contact: **480-358-3719 · Erica.Perez@QueenCreek.org**
- Source: Town of Queen Creek "Seeking Vendors for Spring Events" news release —
  https://www.queencreekaz.gov/Home/Components/News/News/10734/ (also mirrored at
  https://www.queencreekaz.gov/Home/Components/News/News/10200/)

**Worth noting in Q&A prep:** this contact (Erica Perez) is different from McKinna
Evans, who `HANDOVER.md` lists as the vendor contact and homework recipient. Both may
be current — Town staff assignments shift year to year — but confirm which one is
actually correct before your interview so you don't cite a stale name.

This also gives you a real number for the "gap" framing: **3,500+ people attend, but
only a fraction stay for vendors/races** — you can now cite an actual attendance
figure instead of only describing the drop-off qualitatively.

### Town survey precedent (Pillar 3 homework)
Confirmed — the Town already runs resident surveys using the exact mechanism you're
proposing:
- **2025 Citizen Survey**: distributed via postcard with unique ID; residents could
  complete it "online via a link, text, or **QR code**," with a phone option too.
  Administered by Summit Survey Research on the Town's behalf, ~699 residents
  surveyed. — https://explorequeencreek.com/residents-report-overall-satisfaction-with-queen-creek-in-2025-citizen-survey/
- **Community Parks Survey**: similar postcard/unique-ID + phone model, run by
  WestGroup Research. — https://www.queencreekaz.gov/Home/Components/News/News/12076/13
- Town's standing survey info page: https://www.queencreekaz.gov/community/citizen-survey

**This is a strong upgrade to Pillar 3's framing.** You can now say the Town already
uses QR-code/online survey collection for resident feedback — your proposal isn't
introducing new infrastructure, it's **extending an existing, proven practice** to a
single event. That's a materially stronger answer to the "is this really meaningful"
pushback (Q&A Challenge #06) than presenting it as a novel idea.
