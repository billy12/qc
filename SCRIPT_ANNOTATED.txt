Spring Into QC -- Presentation Script (Annotated / Study Version)
================================================================================

Not a script to read -- a study tool. For each slide: what to hit (in your own words),
and WHY THAT'S THE CHOICE, so you can explain the reasoning behind any piece of this
if you're asked, adapt on the fly if you lose your place, or field a curveball without
it derailing you. Fluency means you could rebuild this pitch from memory even if every
slide vanished.

Cross-references: HANDOVER.md (Q&A prep), PROPOSAL_PLANNING.md (open judgment calls),
SOURCES.md (where every number comes from).

================================================================================
SLIDE 1 -- TITLE
================================================================================

SAY: Introduce yourself, name the event, state the one-line frame -- "connecting the
event families already love."

WHY THIS FRAMING, SPECIFICALLY: The previous version said "enhancing engagement beyond
the egg hunt," which signals a problem to fix. This version says "connecting the event
families already love," which signals a strength to build on. That's a meaningfully
different posture -- you're not evaluating whether the event works, you're extending
what already works. Interviewers who built this event will hear the difference. Lead
with it.

================================================================================
SLIDE 2 -- WHO WE'RE SERVING
================================================================================

SAY: Three stats, each with a "so what" tied directly to the proposal: growth rate ->
families still forming traditions; income -> incentive has to match the community;
homeownership -> they'll come back if this year earns it. Close with the Town's own
vision quote.

WHY THREE STATS INSTEAD OF SIX, AND WHY EACH HAS A NARRATIVE LINE: The old version
had six stat boxes and let them speak for themselves. This version picks three and
explains why each one matters to THIS PROPOSAL SPECIFICALLY. That's the difference
between showing homework and showing thinking. A panelist can nod at demographic data;
they remember a candidate who knew WHY that data mattered.

WHY THE INCOME NUMBER ($141,978) GETS PLANTED HERE: You'll use it again in Finding 1
-- "a plain ticket isn't worth advertising on its own, especially in a $142,000-
median-income community." Putting it on this slide first means it reads as EVIDENCE
YOU KNOW THE AUDIENCE, not as a rhetorical flourish dropped into the middle of a
finding.

WHY THE HOMEOWNERSHIP LINE IS THE EMOTIONAL CLOSER: "They'll come back next year if
this year earns it" reframes the entire proposal's stakes. You're not just talking
about one event -- you're talking about whether this town's signature spring event
becomes a tradition or stays a one-off.

================================================================================
SLIDE 3 -- SPRING INTO QC TODAY
================================================================================

SAY: What the event offers (full activity list + 3,500+ attendees), then the gap (egg
hunt is the only goal, nothing connects what comes next).

WHY LEAD WITH WHAT WORKS BEFORE NAMING THE GAP: Town staff built this event. It draws
3,500+ people. Leading with "here's what's working" before "here's the gap" reads as
EVALUATION, not CRITICISM. That's exactly what a panelist is listening for -- can you
critique an existing program without being dismissive of the people who built it?

WHY "NONE OF THEM ARE CONNECTED" IS THE EXACT CLOSING LINE: That phrase is the thesis
of the entire problem. It maps to all three findings and sets up the recommendation
framing ("built on what already works"). Land it cleanly and advance.

================================================================================
SLIDE 4 -- WHAT THE RESEARCH REVEALED
================================================================================

SAY: Finding 1 -- raffle has no real partnership behind it. Finding 2 -- connections
missing, not the content, and the spacing is correct. Finding 3 -- post-hunt moment
unclaimed. Close: elements are strong, they just aren't talking to each other.

WHY FINDING 1 CHANGED FROM "THE RAFFLE LOOP IS BROKEN" TO "THE RAFFLE ISN'T BACKED
BY A REAL PARTNERSHIP": "Broken" is abstract -- what does it tell you about the fix?
"Not backed by a real partnership" is specific and implies the solution before you say
it. It also signals that you understand WHY the ticket has no urgency -- not because
the mechanic is wrong, but because there's nothing behind it worth staying for.

WHY YOU INVOKE THE INCOME NUMBER AGAIN INSIDE FINDING 1: "Especially in a $142,000-
median-income community" connects demographic knowledge to problem analysis. You're not
just reciting a stat -- you're applying it. That's integrated thinking, and it's what
separates a candidate who read the event page from one who actually prepared.

WHY FINDING 2 EXPLICITLY SAYS THE SPACING IS CORRECT: "Fields are intentionally spaced
apart for crowd flow, and that's correct, not the problem." This pre-empts any
assumption that you'd propose redesigning the layout. You understand WHY the site is
built the way it is -- the problem is relational, not structural.

WHY FINDING 3 LANDS LAST: It's the most specific and most vivid -- "the moment right
after a family's hunt ends" is something every parent in the room can picture. It's
also the emotional beat that makes the audience feel the problem before you solve it.

================================================================================
SLIDE 5 -- WHAT THIS IS BUILT ON
================================================================================

SAY: Confirmed facts (event basics, attendance, vendors, demographics), then the three
named assumptions (no raffle prize publicized, flow not documented, departure timing
not tracked), then the one-line data gap note.

WHY SHOW YOUR ASSUMPTIONS INSTEAD OF HIDING THEM: This is the single biggest
credibility move in the deck. Confidently presenting only what supports your
recommendation is what a weak candidate does. Naming what you assumed -- and why --
heads off the data challenge before it's asked and makes you look more rigorous, not
less prepared.

WHY "I'M TREATING THAT AS A REAL GAP, NOT AN OVERSIGHT BY THE TEAM" IS THE EXACT
PHRASING FOR THE RAFFLE ASSUMPTION: You're separating your interpretation from a
judgment about the Town's competence. This distinction matters in a room with the
people who run this event.

WHY FLAGGING "ON-SITE FLOW ISN'T PUBLICLY DOCUMENTED" PROTECTS YOU: If a panelist
asks "have you seen the actual site map?", you've already said you're working from
what's marketed. You haven't overclaimed. The honest answer is: "the concept is sound
given the park's known layout; exact positioning would need a walkthrough with your
events team before finalizing."

================================================================================
SLIDE 6 -- THE RECOMMENDATION OVERVIEW
================================================================================

SAY: Three strategies in one breath -- what each one does -- then the thesis sentence.

WHY THREE STRATEGIES AND HOW THEY MAP TO THE THREE FINDINGS:
  - Strategy 1 (Local Partnerships) -> answers Finding 1 (raffle has no real backing)
  - Strategy 2 (Post-Hunt Moment)   -> answers Finding 3 (that moment is unclaimed)
  - Strategy 3 (Staff Connectivity) -> answers Finding 2 (connections are missing)

The sequencing leads with the prize pool because it's the foundation -- Strategy 2
only works if there's something worth staying for (Strategy 1), and Strategy 3 only
works if there's anticipation to direct (Strategy 2).

THE THESIS SENTENCE TO KNOW COLD: "Strategy 1 gives them something worth winning.
Strategy 2 gives them a reason to stay for it. Strategy 3 turns that into a reason
to explore." If you remember nothing else verbatim from this deck, remember this.
It's the answer to "why do these three strategies belong together."

WHY "BUILT ON WHAT ALREADY WORKS" IS THE LOAD-BEARING FRAME: Every strategy is tied
to existing infrastructure -- the raffle that already runs, the hunt that already
happens, the walkway that already connects every zone. You are not adding, you are
ACTIVATING. That framing is your defense against "isn't this incremental?"

================================================================================
SLIDE 7 -- STRATEGY 1: STRENGTHEN LOCAL PARTNERSHIPS
================================================================================

SAY: Name the five businesses and what each contributes. Explain how the prize pool
works (announced 9 AM, drawn 12:15 PM, zero Town cost). Then Silver Eggs -- inside
the existing hunt, bonus tickets, sets up Strategy 2.

WHY NAMING THE SPECIFIC BUSINESSES CHANGES THE ROOM: Queen Creek Olive Mill is
"Arizona's only working olive mill." Schnepf Farms is "the largest peach grower in the
State." These are not generic sponsors -- they are IDENTITY BUSINESSES that define
what Queen Creek is. Naming them turns "we'd find a sponsor" from an assumption into
evidence of real homework. You don't need to read the list -- but drop two or three
names naturally when you talk about the prize pool.

WHY "PROSPECTIVE PARTNERS, NOT CONFIRMED COMMITMENTS" IS ON THE SLIDE: Intellectual
honesty. If a panelist asks "have you spoken to these businesses?", the answer is
"these are proposed partners -- a confirmed ask would follow approval of the
framework." That's a credible answer. Overpromising commitments you don't have is not.

WHY SILVER EGGS GO INSIDE THE EXISTING HUNT INSTEAD OF AS A SEPARATE ACTIVITY: Zero
new operational burden for the hunt itself. Families are already hunting -- Silver Eggs
are a natural element hidden alongside existing prizes, not a new checklist bolted on.
"Natural fit, not a new mechanic" is the exact language worth using if this gets
challenged.

WHY SILVER EGGS "DO DOUBLE DUTY" FOR STRATEGY 2: One mechanic serves two strategies.
That efficiency is worth naming -- it shows the proposal is integrated, not three
separate ideas bundled together.

================================================================================
SLIDE 8 -- STRATEGY 2: CLAIM THE POST-HUNT MOMENT
================================================================================

SAY: The moment this strategy has to claim (hunt ends, nothing next, default is to
leave). How Silver Eggs claim it (family doesn't know until they check; 12:15 PM
drawing is real Queen Creek prizes). The core idea: they don't need a new reason to
stay -- they need to realize they might already have one.

WHY "FAMILIES DON'T NEED A NEW REASON TO STAY -- THEY NEED TO REALIZE THEY MIGHT
ALREADY HAVE ONE" IS THE EMOTIONAL CENTER OF THIS PRESENTATION: Every other strategy
adds something. This one REFRAMES something that already exists. That reframe is more
powerful, and it's the insight that makes the whole deck hang together. If you feel
the room lean in anywhere, it'll be here. Don't rush it.

WHY THE SILVER EGG UNCERTAINTY IS A FEATURE, NOT A BUG: A family doesn't know if
they're holding a live raffle ticket until they check their bucket. That uncertainty is
what creates the "let's see" moment -- it's the reason to pause instead of walk out.
A guaranteed ticket would skip that beat entirely.

WHY NAMING THE SPECIFIC PRIZES AGAIN HERE MATTERS: "An Olive Mill family tasting, a
Schnepf Farms day pass, a San Tan Flat dinner" reinforces that the prize is GENUINELY
QUEEN CREEK'S OWN, not a gift card. The specificity is what makes "let's see if we
won" feel worth saying. Generic prizes don't do that.

================================================================================
SLIDE 9 -- STRATEGY 3: STRENGTHEN CONNECTIVITY THROUGH STAFF
================================================================================

SAY: Strategy 2 gives them a reason to want to stay -- Strategy 3 turns that into
families actually moving through the site. The Spine (vendor row + walkway) already
connects every zone. Staff give a five-second handoff at each exit, built around the
ticket. Egg Hunt Field exit is the priority. No new infrastructure -- the fix is what
connects the zones, not the fences.

WHY STAFF CONNECTIVITY BEATS A LAYOUT REDESIGN: An earlier version of this proposal
considered restructuring the site into a guided circular path. That was a design fix
-- it works, but it requires infrastructure decisions and physical changes to the event
footprint. This version keeps the fields exactly as they are (spaced for crowd flow,
which is correct) and fixes the problem through staff behavior and a shared identity.
That's a lighter, faster, more politically workable solution -- and it's more honest
about what the candidate can actually change in their first year.

WHY "REDEPLOYMENT" IS A WORD TO AVOID HERE: If you say "we'd redeploy staff," a panel
with special events experience will hear "you're moving people away from their assigned
posts" -- which implies a total event redesign and signals you don't understand how
events are staffed. The correct framing is: staff are already stationed at each zone
exit; we're adding a connectivity handoff to what they're already doing. "No added
positions, no added hours" is the answer. Nobody moves. The fix is what they SAY, not
where they stand.

WHY THE FIVE-SECOND HANDOFF SCRIPT MATTERS: "You've got a shot at a prize -- have you
checked out the races yet?" is specific enough to train on. A vague instruction
("point families to other activities") gets ignored under event-day pressure. A short,
specific line that any staff member can repeat verbatim does not.

WHY THE EGG HUNT FIELD EXIT IS NAMED AS THE PRIORITY: It's the highest-volume handoff
and the most time-critical -- families finishing the hunt are at peak Silver-Egg
excitement from Strategy 2. That's the moment. The full operational plan also flags
Zone A exit as a crowd-safety priority -- which means the staffed handoff is part of
the event's safety footprint, not just a retention idea. Worth one sentence if the
moment allows it.

WHY "BUILT FROM REAL EVENT FOOTAGE, NOT JUST MARKETING COPY" IS ON THE SLIDE: It
signals you watched actual event content -- not just the Parks & Rec event page. In a
municipal interview, candidates who demonstrate genuine research stand out. If asked
what footage, be ready to describe what you saw specifically.

================================================================================
SLIDE 10 -- FINAL RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================

SAY: Recap all three strategies in one sentence each. "No new infrastructure
required." Mention the companion documents. Open to questions.

WHY "NO NEW INFRASTRUCTURE REQUIRED" IS THE CLOSING ANCHOR PHRASE: It answers every
unspoken cost, staffing, and complexity objection in four words. Everything in this
proposal -- the prize pool, the Silver Eggs, the staff handoff, the walkway -- uses
something that already exists. That's the promise. Land it last and cleanly.

WHY THE COMPANION DOCUMENT REFERENCE IS PHRASED AS "AVAILABLE," NOT "COMING": The
operational plan (PDF) and budget (Excel) are ready. Say "available as companion
documents" -- not "we're working on it." You have the depth; the presentation kept it
clean.

CLOSING BEAT: This is your last impression. Recap the three strategies without
rushing, say the infrastructure line, mention the documents, and stop. Let the Q&A
open naturally.
