## The Recommendation We Built

**Direction chosen: Two connected pillars, focused on community engagement at the event itself.** (A third idea — closing the "why do families leave early" data gap with a formal feedback survey — was deliberately demoted to a one-line forward-looking mention. It is *not* one of the two core recommendations; see the note under Pillar framing below.)

### Pillar 1 — Redesign the Journey (Event Layout & Participant Journey)
Restructure the site from a series of disconnected stops into one guided circular path: controlled Entrance A/B → Great Egg Walk → Vendor Row → Central Egg Hunt (physical anchor of the site) → Animal Races → Periphery Loop (Play Zone, Tot Spot, Color Blast on the *return route*, not off to the side). No new attractions — a resequencing of the existing footprint so every family passes every vendor and attraction before the exit.

### Pillar 2 — Gamify Retention (Gamification & Retention Incentives)
Expand the raffle ticket mechanic that already exists into four layers: a Grand Prize Drawing announced at event open (not sprung at noon) and drawn at 12:15 PM; a combined punch + bingo card (punches for periphery attractions, bingo squares for vendor/partner interactions) handed out at the entrance; hidden golden eggs scattered venue-wide for bonus tickets; and a bracket-champion bonus tied to the existing race-pick mechanic.

**Why these two together:** Pillar 1 gets families moving through the whole site. Pillar 2 gives them a reason to keep moving. Each pillar traces back to one of the three research findings below (see `SCRIPT_ANNOTATED.md` for the exact mapping).

**Data-gap idea, demoted on purpose:** an earlier draft treated "close the data gap with a formal feedback survey" as a third, equal pillar. Bill corrected that — the assignment is about a community-engagement enhancement, not a data-collection project. It now appears only as: (1) one sentence on the Research Foundation slide noting it as a natural future step, and (2) one closing-line callback on the Final Recommendation slide. It has no dedicated slide, no Operational Plan line items, and no budget line.

## Three Key Research Findings (Slide 4 in the deck)

1. **The raffle loop is broken** — tickets exist, no announced drawing → addressed by Pillar 2
2. **Activities run in silos** — egg hunts, races, Play Zone, EggScape are not connected by any event-wide narrative or flow → addressed by Pillar 1
3. **The post-hunt moment is unclaimed** — egg hunts are already staggered well; the problem is per-wave drift after each group finishes, not one mass exodus → absorbed into Pillar 1's physical redesign (the periphery-loop placement *is* the structural answer to this finding, replacing what was originally a much smaller "station a staffer at the exit" idea)
